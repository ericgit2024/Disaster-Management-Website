Cyclone Information

Understanding and Preparing for Cyclones

What are Cyclones?

Cyclones are large-scale air masses that rotate around a strong center of low atmospheric pressure. They are characterized by inward spiraling winds that rotate counterclockwise in the Northern Hemisphere and clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere.

Tropical Cyclones

Form over warm ocean waters, including hurricanes and typhoons.

Extratropical Cyclones

Form outside the tropics with frontal boundaries between warm and cold air masses.

Subtropical Cyclones

Have characteristics of both tropical and extratropical cyclones.

Mesocyclones

Smaller-scale rotation within thunderstorms that can lead to tornadoes.

Category 1-2

Minimal to Moderate damage. Winds 119-177 km/h

Category 3

Extensive damage. Winds 178-208 km/h

Category 4-5

Extreme to Catastrophic damage. Winds >209 km/h

Warning Signs

  • Rapidly falling barometric pressure
  • Increasing wind speeds and gusty conditions
  • Large ocean swells and rough seas
  • Dark, threatening clouds
  • Heavy rainfall and thunderstorms
  • Storm surge warnings

Before a Cyclone

  • Create an emergency plan for your family
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, and medical supplies
  • Secure loose items outside your home
  • Trim trees and branches that could fall on your house
  • Know your evacuation routes and local shelters
  • Monitor weather updates and official warnings

During a Cyclone

  • Stay indoors and away from windows, skylights, and glass doors
  • Take shelter in the strongest part of your house (typically a small interior room)
  • Keep a battery-powered radio for emergency updates
  • Turn off utilities if instructed to do so
  • Do not go outside during the "eye" of the cyclone - severe winds will return
  • If flooding occurs, move to higher ground

After a Cyclone

  • Continue listening to emergency information and alerts
  • Check your property for damage and stay out of damaged buildings
  • Avoid wading in floodwater which may contain dangerous debris
  • Be aware of dangerous power lines, broken gas lines, and other hazards
  • Document property damage with photographs for insurance claims
  • Help neighbors who may require special assistance
