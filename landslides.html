Landslide Information

Understanding and Preparing for Landslides

What are Landslides?

  • Landslides are the movement of rock, earth, or debris down a sloped section of land.
  • They are caused by rain, earthquakes, volcanoes, or other factors that make the slope unstable.

Rockfalls

Abrupt movements of rocks and boulders that detach from steep slopes.

Debris flows

Rapid movement of loose soil, rock, and water that moves downslope as a slurry.

Mudflows

Fast-moving flow of water-saturated earth materials that moves downslope.

Earthflows

Downslope movement of soil and weathered rock when saturated with water.

Creep

Slow, continuous downward movement of soil or rock on a slope.

Warning Signs

  • Springs, seeps, or saturated ground in areas that have not typically been wet before
  • New cracks or unusual bulges in the ground, street pavements, or sidewalks
  • Soil moving away from foundations
  • Tilting or cracking of concrete floors and foundations
  • Broken water lines and other underground utilities
  • Leaning telephone poles, trees, retaining walls, or fences
  • Rapid increase in creek water levels

Before a Landslide

  • Learn about landslide risk in your area
  • Watch for warning signs like unusual sounds, shifting foundations, or new cracks in walls
  • Create an emergency plan and evacuation route
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials
  • Install flexible pipe fittings to avoid gas and water leaks
  • Plant ground cover on slopes and retaining walls where needed
  • Consider contacting local officials about protective measures

During a Landslide

  • If indoors, stay inside and take cover under a sturdy piece of furniture
  • If outdoors, move quickly away from the path of the landslide
  • Watch for flooding, which often accompanies landslides
  • Listen for unusual sounds that might indicate moving debris
  • Be especially alert when driving - watch for collapsed pavement and mud

After a Landslide

  • Stay away from the slide area - there may be danger of additional slides
  • Check for injured or trapped people near the slide area
  • Help neighbors who may require special assistance
  • Listen to local alerts for emergency information
  • Report broken utility lines to appropriate authorities
  • Check building foundations, chimneys, and surrounding land for damage
  • Replant damaged ground as soon as possible to prevent further erosion
